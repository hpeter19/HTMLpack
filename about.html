ABOUT

Sauti Sol is a renowned Kenyan Afropop band formed in Nairobi in 2005 by Bien-Aimé Baraza, Willis Chimano, and Savara Mudigi, initially as an a cappella group. Guitarist Polycarp Otieno later joined, completing the quartet. They debuted with Mwanzo (2008), followed by Sol Filosofia (2011), which earned critical acclaim and awards. Their 2015 album, Live and Die in Afrika, was released as a free download, boosting their popularity. Known for hits like "Sura Yako" and "Melanin," Sauti Sol has toured Africa, Europe, and the U.S., collaborating with artists like Spoek Mathambo and Ladysmith Black Mambazo. They’ve won multiple awards (Kisima, BET, MTV EMA) and gained global recognition while maintaining strong Kenyan roots. The band members, all university graduates, met in school and refined their sound at Alliance Française before rising to fame.

Sauti Sol has cemented its place as one of Africa’s most influential Afropop bands, blending rich harmonies, vibrant rhythms, and socially conscious lyrics. From their a cappella roots to global stardom, their journey reflects innovation and cultural pride. With multiple awards, international collaborations, and chart-topping hits, they continue to elevate Kenyan music worldwide. Their success inspires aspiring artists, proving that authenticity and creativity can break barriers and resonate across continents.
